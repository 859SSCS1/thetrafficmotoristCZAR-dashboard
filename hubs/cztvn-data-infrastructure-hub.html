Real-time flow & incidents

TomTom Traffic API ↗ Live flow (observed speeds, travel times) and incidents — closures, construction, crashes — refreshed roughly every 30–60 seconds. Free developer tier with a key. → dashboard: the live congestion + incident layer on the map Dev-keyRented HERE Traffic API ↗ Flow, incidents, and a congestion-severity score, sourced heavily from delivery and trucking fleets — strongest on freight corridors and interstates. Freemium developer access. → dashboard: freight-corridor speed overlay; cross-check against TomTom Dev-keyRented Google Routes API ↗ Traffic-aware routing and travel times, priced per request with a monthly free credit. Broad urban coverage from phone signals. → dashboard: live-vs-typical travel-time deltas per corridor Dev-keyRented Mapbox Traffic Data ↗ Sells the raw speed dataset — live and typical-by-time-of-week — matched to OpenStreetMap. Free tier for SDK use; the dataset itself is licensable (see Rung 3). The closest of the four to data you can analyze, not just display. → dashboard: the typical-week baseline live speeds get compared against Dev-keyRented

City-cycle benchmarks

INRIX Global Traffic Scorecard ↗ The definitive city-cycle benchmark: three years of data across nearly 1,000 cities — per-driver hours lost, congestion cost, busiest corridors, and each metro's commute pattern. The full report is a free download. This is most of what you need to speak with authority on any city's cycle. FreeReport TomTom Traffic Index ↗ Live and historical congestion rankings by city, including Atlanta. Already on the newsdesk hub — here it pairs with INRIX as the second cross-city yardstick. FreeReport

Free government feeds

State 511 / DOT feed ↗ Every state runs one; Georgia 511 is the local instance. Free real-time incidents, closures, and construction — no key, no storage restriction. FreeOwnable FHWA Highway Statistics ↗ Licensed drivers by age, vehicle-miles traveled, the authoritative annual tables — the national denominator behind any per-driver claim. FreeOwnable Bureau of Transportation Statistics ↗ Broad federal mobility data for a deeper cut — freight, modal splits, long-run series. FreeOwnable

Structural context — the “why” behind the cycle

Census OnTheMap (LEHD / LODES) ↗ Home-to-work commute origin-destination down to the census-block level. Free. This is the structural reason a metro's peak looks the way it does. FreeOwnable Census — data.census.gov (ACS) ↗ Commute times, mode share, and non-driver demographics by geography — the human layer under the traffic numbers. FreeOwnable GTFS & GTFS-Realtime ↗ Open transit schedule plus live vehicle feeds. Transit ridership shifts explain much of a metro's road cycle — the multimodal half of the picture. FreeOwnable
Rung 02

Agency Access

know now · partner into later

Partnership-gated · free to qualifying agencies

NPMRDS via RITIS ↗ The FHWA probe-speed dataset for the national highway network, delivered through RITIS (University of Maryland CATT Lab). Free to qualifying public agencies and researchers — the gate is status, not price. AgencyGated Waze for Cities ↗ A free, two-way partnership giving public agencies crowdsourced speeds, slowdowns, and incidents — in exchange for sharing closures back. Agency standing is the key that opens it. AgencyGated
Rung 03

The First Transaction

the milestone rung · spend begins here

Threshold · the account opens here

This is where TM CZAR Television Network, Inc. makes its first transaction.

Not a shopping list — an earned graduation. The dashboard, built and proven on Rung 1, is what justifies crossing this line.

One distinction to carry through every card below: paid access and paid ownership are not the same thing. Higher TomTom / HERE / Google tiers still only rent you access — more calls, but you still can't store or redistribute. The transactions that move you toward a proprietary asset are the data-licensing ones. The first spend should point at something you can keep.

Buy something you own

Mapbox Traffic Data — license ↗ License the raw speed dataset outright via S3/GCS delivery — you keep it and reprocess it. The clearest “buy something you own” candidate for a first transaction. PaidOwnable StreetLight InSight ↗ Licensed origin-destination, volumes, VMT, vehicle-hours of delay, and 15-minute intersection movements for nearly every road in North America. Enterprise/agency pricing; the industry-standard pattern platform. PaidOwnable

Rent broader access

INRIX — analytics & real-time ↗ Beyond the free Scorecard: real-time feeds and deeper analytics. This tier is access, not ownership — the terms don't let you store or redistribute. PaidRented Replica ↗ Enterprise activity- and travel-pattern modeling. Platform access, priced for agencies and large operators. PaidRented TomTom / HERE / Google — paid tiers ↗ Higher call volumes and service levels on the same APIs from Rung 1. More of what you're renting — the terms still forbid storing or redistributing the data. PaidRented

The north star — the layer you build, not buy

CMMD corridor capture ↗ Your own instrumented capture — the one data layer you fully own, and the endgame the whole ladder points at. Not a purchase; a build. Every rented feed above is a placeholder until this exists. OwnedOwnable

The ladder logic: master the free tier → build the dashboard as proof → let that proof justify the first paid transaction → point that transaction at ownership, not rental. Every rung earns the next.
